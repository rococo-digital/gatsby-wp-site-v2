Arranging or facilitating commission of a child sex offence

Arranging or facilitating commission of a child sex offence is an offence contrary to section 14 of the Sexual Offences Act 2003. It is an offence for a person to intentionally arrange or facilitate something that they intend to do, intends another person to do, or believes that another person will so, in any part of the world, and in doing so would involve the commission of an offence.

Penalties

If convicted of arranging or facilitating commission of a child sex offence you could face:

  • A maximum sentence of 14 years imprisonment.
  • You will be ordered to complete the Notification Requirements of the Sex Offenders Register.
Defences

Defences vary depending on the particular circumstances of your case and our specialist advice is tailored to you. Defences can include:

  • that a person was acting for the protection of a child.

Meeting a child following sexual grooming

Meeting a child following sexual grooming is an offence contrary to section 15 of the Sexual Offences Act 2003. A person, aged 18 or over, commits an offence of meeting a child following sexual grooming if they have met or communicated with a child who is under 16 years old, and the person does not reasonably believe that the child is 16 years or over, on at least two occasions and subsequently:

  • Intentionally meets the child.
  • Travels with the intention of meeting the child in any part of the world.
  • Arranges to meet the child in any part of the world.
  • The child travels with the intention of meeting in any part of the world.
  • Intends to do anything to, or in respect of the child and during or after the meeting and in any part of the world, which if done will involve the commission an offence.
Penalties

If convicted of arranging or facilitating commission of a child sex offence you could face:

  • A maximum sentence of 10 years imprisonment.
  • You will be ordered to complete the Notification Requirements of the Sex Offenders Register.
Defences

Defences vary depending on the particular circumstances of your case and our specialist advice is tailored to you. Defences can include:

  • A defence can be that you had a reasonable belief that the child was 16 years or over.

Sexual Communication with a Child

Sexual Communication with a child is an offence contrary to section 15A of the Sexual Offences Act 2003. It is an offence for a person aged 18 years or over to communicate with a child who is under 16 years old and the person does not reasonably believe that the child is 16 years or over if:

  • It is for the purpose of obtaining sexual gratification.
  • The communication is sexual or is intended to encourage a child to make a communication that is sexual.

Communication is ‘sexual’ if any part of it relates to sexual activity or a reasonable person would, consider any part of the communication to be sexual, regardless of any person’s purpose.

Penalties

If convicted of arranging or facilitating commission of a child sex offence you could face:

  • A maximum sentence of 2 years imprisonment.
  • You will be ordered to complete the Notification Requirements of the Sex Offenders Register.
Defences

Defences vary depending on the particular circumstances of your case and our specialist advice is tailored to you. Defences can include:

  • A defence can be that you had a reasonable belief that the child was 16 years or over.
The sex offenders’ register

Anyone convicted of a sexual grooming offence is added to the sex offenders’ register and being subject to a Sexual Offences Prevention Order, which places strict restrictions on the individual such as not approaching or associating with children.
